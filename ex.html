A proper glass will make any wine taste better

Lets set the record straight, you can drink wine out of a toilet bowl if you wanted, but any proper tasing of wine comes with a proper set of wine goblets. This is because with the varying types of wine goblets made each has a purpose and tradition. However it is to note, the effect of glass shape on the taste of wine has not been demonstrated by any scientific study and remains controversial.

It is however believed by some that the shape of the glass is important, as it concentrates the flavour and aroma (or bouquet) to emphasize the varietal's characteristic.

Step 2 of 6

Wine tastes better served slightly cool

So you have your wine goblets ready, now what?

Well, for starters all wine should be stored at the same temperature, regardless of its color. But reds and whites are consumed at quite different temperatures. Too often people drink white wines too cold and red wines too warm, limiting how much you can enjoy the wine.

*A white that’s too cold will be flavorless and a red that’s too warm is often flabby and alcoholic. Here is a key to ideal wine service temperatures:

Ideal Wine Temperatures

  • Red Wine: 11-20 °C
  • White Wine: 4-14 °F

Opening Your First Bottle Of Wine

While there are many methods to uncorking a bottle of wine, there are definitly wrong ways in doing so. This method will allow you to safely and properly uncork your Vino.

Close
  • Make sure you have a corkscrew availiable.
  1. Place the knife below the lip of the bottle and cut.
  2. Set the screw just off center and insert, rotating straight into the cork.
  3. Continue to screw into the cork until only one curl remains.
  4. Lever on the first step
  5. Lever on the second, finally easing the cork out with your hand.

Step 4 of 6

Pouring Your First Glass

First, it is important to note, if your pouring for guests then wine should be presented with the label facing the individual. Proper etiquette recommends pouring for the women first, and starting with the eldest woman at the table and filling clockwise. Then the men are served.

The glasses should be poured approximately halfway, and then the bottle should be twisted when finished to avoid wine dripping on the table. Red and white wines should be poured into the center of the glass, while sparkling wines and champagne should be served into a tilted glass to keep the bubbles intact.

How to Hold your Wine Glass

The Way You Hold Your Glass Shows Your Class

Close

Close
Close

There is etiquette to holding a wine glass. Above all, it should be steady and comfortable in your hand. But etiquette dictates one should hold a glass by the stem or base, not by the bowl. Doing so avoids unsightly fingerprints, and if you hold a glass by the bowl, your body temperature can affect the temperature of the wine—often for the worse. Not only that, it can also be easier to swirl and look at the wine when you're holding the glass the correct way.

Step 6 of 6

It's time to take your first sip. But First...

The following wine tasting tips are practiced by sommeliers to refine their palates and sharpen their ability to recall wines. Even though this method is used by pro’s, it’s actually quite simple to understand and can help anyone to improve their wine palate.

Look

Check out the color, opacity and viscosity (wine legs). You don’t really need to spend more than 5 seconds on this step.

Close

Smell

Pick out at least 2 flavors and take your time identifying them. There are 3 types of wine aromas:

Primary Aromas come from grapes and include fruit, herb and flower notes

Secondary Aromas come from fermentation and yeast aromas.

Tertiary Bouquets come from aging, oxidation and oak such as baking spices, nutty aromas and vanilla.

Close

Taste

Two elements make up taste: flavor and structure.

Flavors such as lemon, raspberry or coconut.

Structure such as the level of sweetness, body, alcohol, acidity, and tannin.

Profile The taste of wine is also time-based, there is a beginning, middle (mid-palate) and end (finish).

Close

Conclude

Did the wine taste balanced or out of balance? Did you like the wine? Was this wine unique or unmemorable? Were there any characteristics that shined through and impressed you?
